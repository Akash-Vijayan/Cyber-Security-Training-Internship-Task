1) What is DNSRecon?

DNSRecon is a powerful DNS enumeration tool used to perform
reconnaissance on DNS records of a target domain.
It automates the process of gathering DNS information, checking zone
transfers, brute-forcing subdomains, and more.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

2) Why use DNSRecon?

-   Collects comprehensive DNS record types (A, MX, NS, TXT, SOA, etc.).
-   Detects misconfigured DNS servers (e.g., zone transfer
    vulnerabilities).
-   Performs subdomain brute-forcing and reverse lookups.
-   Helps in penetration testing and security assessments.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

3) How does DNSRecon work?

1.  You provide a domain name.
2.  DNSRecon queries DNS servers to retrieve records.
3.  It can:
    -   Perform zone transfers.
    -   Check for cached DNS records.
    -   Brute-force subdomains using wordlists.
    -   Do reverse lookups on IP ranges.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

5) Practical Workflow

1.  Basic DNS info:

    dnsrecon -d target.com -t std

2.  Check zone transfer vulnerability:

    dnsrecon -d target.com -t axfr

3.  Subdomain brute force (with wordlist):

    dnsrecon -d target.com -D wordlist.txt -t brt

------------------------------------------------------------------------

6) Limitations

-   Results depend on target DNS configuration.
-   Privacy-protected domains may reveal limited info.
-   Aggressive scans (brute force) may be detected as malicious
    activity.
